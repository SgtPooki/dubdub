OMNICHANNEL MARKETING

Driving Engagement & Revenue Through Personalization
< View All Resources

Modern retailers understand the rewards of delivering the most engaging digital shopping experiences to today’s connected consumer. Engagement is the key to staying relevant and top-of-mind in the crowded marketplace. It can drive online and offline revenue and help retailers stay competitive with the likes of Amazon, for example.

But consistently delivering high levels of convenience, selection, service and customer-centric experiences is challenging. Exploring the myriad of complex and often expensive technology options available can also be daunting. Focusing on a single concept that aligns with business goals can be an effective way to sort through the options and identify the most impactful initiative. Present and future technology solutions will present themselves.

Personalization through highly contextual marketing, communications and promotions

Let’s explore the concept of personalization and the goal of delivering highly contextual marketing campaigns as part of a larger omnichannel marketing strategy. Contextualization is at the heart of delivering personalized experiences. Existing identity factors like a shopper’s age, gender and demographic can be further enhanced by additional contextual cues such as:

  • The exact time a shopper enters a store and the location of that store
  • The time a shopper accesses a website, the device they are using, as well as his or her current location
  • A shopper’s past browsing, add-to-cart, wish list and purchase history

Providing a particular promotional message to a shopper using their smartphone in a particular location at a particular time is powerful from the marketing and communication perspectives. Experiences like this can drive instant purchases, repeat purchases, increase brand loyalty and produce a slew of additional marketing benefits like increased exposure and brand sharing through social networks.

Contextualization begets personalization when retailers can leverage shopping history. For example, website visitors who are first-time purchasers might qualify for a 10% discount on selected products, but returning shoppers identified as high-value customers could qualify for an even larger discount of 25% so retailers can cultivate greater brand loyalty.

While contextualized offers can depend on dynamic factors such as a consumer’s device, at the same time these offers must be aligned with the full spectrum of marketing communications that a customer receives. In other words, for the best user experience retailers should look to ‘layer in’ contextual incentives, such as promotions, coupons, discounts, cross-sells, up-sells etc.

Technology Needs

Modern cloud-based technologies and commerce platforms are developed to enable highly personalized experiences. They’re also designed to integrate completely with common CRMs, email marketing software and marketing automation systems. Through this technology, retailers can layer in contextualization to their customer segmentation and personalization efforts, cutting through the competitive clutter and showing shoppers that they committed to delivering the best shopping experiences possible.
